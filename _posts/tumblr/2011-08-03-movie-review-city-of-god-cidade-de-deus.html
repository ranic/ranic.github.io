In a nutshell, City of God is the precursor to Slumdog Millionaire. However, it is a hundred times rawer, a thousand times more realistic, and a million times more fast paced. In a way, it’s a fusion of Slumdog Millionaire and Scarface, telling the story of both a young power-monger’s self-destructive transformation into druglord of his poverty stricken city and the parallel one of an adolescent who refuses to resign himself to his destiny as a “hood”, seeking a more legitimate lifestyle. These two starkly contrasting paths are so tightly wound that they can be recounted in flawless, linear fashion. If you’re not already convinced, let me give you three reasons why this is one of the greatest movies of all time.

First of all, it’s incredibly dense with action and character development. The story is told from the point-of-view of a very involved narrator, one who understands which information is important and which isn’t. The result is a film in which every minute is crucial, packed with information. Secondly, it’s impossible to hate any one character. Though some are undoubtedly more brutal than others, there is never a moment that suggests that their actions are “inhuman”. In fact, I believe that the sheer humanness of the entire film can be overwhelming at points, a testament to its realism. Lastly, the actors are absolutely fantastic. As trite a platitude as this is often considered, their portrayals of the characters literally transport you to the world that you’re viewing. I also recommend watching the movie in its original Portuguese form with English subtitles; it’s so much more epic.

Very highly recommended. 5/5 stars.Â
