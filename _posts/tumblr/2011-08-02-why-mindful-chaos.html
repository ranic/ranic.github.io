Hey everyone!

For all my die-hard fans (the one or two of you out there), I’m sorry that I haven’t posted much on here recently. Today, I’ll make up for my absence with several posts, beginning with this one. Enjoy!

Mindful: bearing in mind, inclined to be aware (Merriam-Webster)

Chaos: the confused, unorganized state of primordial (M-W)

So why did I chose this cacophonous verbal juxtaposition as my blog’s heading? Well first, in hipster spirit, I needed a combination of words that’s vaguely thought-provoking but not in the cliche way. However, even I’m not so shallow as to mindlessly conform to this obscurely trite veneer of intellectualism (OK, enough thesaurus.com jargon.) In reality, I had two reasons (aside from the one above) for choosing Mindful Chaos as my blog’s title: 

First: I like the duality of it. In an out-of-body, synesthetic experience, I visualized ‘chaos’ as an all-powerful yet utterly intractable creature of darkness. (Give me a break, after a childhood of anime and cartoons, this just resonated with me.) On its own, chaos razes everything in sight – if it can even be called a 'being’, it is one of mindless destruction. Only those who are truly mindful can peacefully observe the existence of chaos and accept its presence. This duality isn’t quite as black and white as I’ve described - different levels of chaos and mindfulness pervade our universe - but it’s a neat model.

Second: Ever since I’ve come across the word and its true definition, I’ve sought to increase my own levels of mindfulness (to over 9000, perhaps?). However, since we live in such a crazy and chaotic world, everything I learn is sucked into the hopelessly tangled mess that is my mind. I created this blog to untangle this knot of 17 years, bit by bit, with the belief that I can one day tame the chaotic beast of my world. 

And you thought I was a hipster before. :)
