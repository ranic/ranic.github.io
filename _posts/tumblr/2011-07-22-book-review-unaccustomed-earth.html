Yesterday, right before I sold my soul to tumblr, I finished reading Jhumpa Lahiri’s Unaccustomed Earth. Already a Pulitzer Prize winner for her earlier short story collection, Interpreter of Maladies, Bengali-English author Lahiri once again paints a vivid portrait of the immigrant world, of life away from one’s roots. The writing itself is flawless and ornate, but that’s not what impresses me. The book consists of eight (for the most part) unrelated vignettes. Each one takes a completely different perspective on the themes that unify the book: separation, longing, redefining ‘home’. It amazes me how Lahiri can conjure so precisely the feelings of a little boy growing up in a foreign country, while simultaneously waxing eloquent on the trials of a man approaching old age. Her ability to shift perspectives, doing so in a manner that’s both artistic and concise, makes her the masterÂ of the modern short story genre.

5/5 stars. I highly recommend this book.
